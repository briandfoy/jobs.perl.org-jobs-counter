Software Developer 
SOM Institute of Genome Science 
University of Maryland, Baltimore, Baltimore, MD 
Requisition Number:  
The Software Developer position is an exciting opportunity to join the
Informatics Department at the new Institute for Genome Sciences, a world
class institute to support genomic, basic and translational research to
improve human health at the University of Maryland, School of Medicine and
beyond. We intend to accomplish this goal by leveraging the expertise of
our domain experts, computational scientists, and engineers, a
state-of-the-art computational facility and our partnerships with other
universities and organizations. Primary responsibilities for this position
include the development of software and database tools for generation,
display and analysis of genomic data.

Essential Functions:
•Maintain daily operation of an engineered analytical system, including
preparing queries, extracting data requested by researchers, and arraying
data in prescribed formats.
•Take requests for system queries or for enhancements of existing
analytical or reporting software and assess requirements for additional
software engineering to accomplish task.
•Consulting with managers or senior staff, translate requirements into
software specifications. 
•Using third generation and advanced programming languages and tools,
accomplish specified upgrade, data query or analysis request, or
enhancement of operating protocol.
•Confer with scientists or senior staff in unit to determine how scientific
inquiries or analyses can be performed more efficiently using current
analytical or operating systems and the software that interacts with them.
•Scan other scientific sites performing similar research and analytical
activities and track use of innovative tools and methods that are in the
published research and in the public domain for possible use in local
methods and software development


Required skills:
Minimum Requirements:
•Knowledge of Perl and object-oriented programming in Perl
•Bachelor’s degree in Bioinformatics, Computer Science, Computer

education.
•Ability to operate in a UNIX/Linux operating environment.
•Knowledge of C++, C#, Java, and one or more relational database management
systems.
•Familiarity with web technologies such as HTML, CSS, PHP, Ajax.

•Ability to support production data management and software pipeline

•Familiarity with object-oriented analysis and programming.
•Familiarity with bioinformatics tools such as sequence similarity,
