Description:
This is a fantastic opportunity to work on the development of all aspects
of the Virus Bulletin website. This role involves working on the look and
feel of our website as well as the back end programming and maintenance.
You will have shared responsibility for the website, working closely with
the Web Developer to ensure a fully functioning, frequently updated and
contemporary commercial website that serves to promote Virus Bulletin’s
products and services.

You will have prior experience of running a website, working in HTML, CSS
and ideally Perl. You’ll have Unix/Linux systems administration skills and
will be able to demonstrate a good understanding of web programming
security issues. You’ll also have the ability to pick up new skills quickly
and will enjoy the opportunity for learning that this position presents.

This is an opportunity to gain new skills and experience and to identify
opportunities to improve our website. Working within the small Virus
Bulletin team, you will have exposure to the whole business as well as the
opportunity to progress your career as you contribute to our continued
success. You are likely to be a creative individual with the ability to
work methodically to ensure that work is carried out to schedule.

This is a part-time role of 22.5 hours per week. The position is based at
our headquarters in Abingdon, Oxfordshire.

Main Duties
• Maintain and develop a fresh and contemporary look and feel to the Virus
Bulletin website
• Update the site on a daily basis
• Suggest and implement ideas to enhance and extend the current
functionality of the website
• Implement proactive methods to increase traffic to the website, including
new functions and features
• Collect data about visitors to the website and volume of site traffic for
analysis
• Maintain a fully automated subscription service including direct online
payment, automated delivery of subscription and automated delivery of
standard communication
• Work on projects in Object Oriented Perl in a Unix/Apache/ mod-perl
environment
• Construct small CGI applications
• Write documentation for the website and be responsible for the
troubleshooting, fixing and mitigating of faults
• Ensure and improve the quality of the code base with respect to coding
style, object oriented design, security and reliability
• Contribute to the achievement of Virus Bulletin’s team objectives
• Assist in the running and maintenance of Virus Bulletin’s anti-spam test
network

• Use of Perl,	HTML and CSS
