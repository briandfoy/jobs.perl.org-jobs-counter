Job Description:
The Systems Analyst Programmer provides programming support and routine
systems analysis and design to the Arnold Library and Internet Services. 
The Systems Analyst Programmer works under limited technical direction and
performs routine programming duties such as coding, testing, and revising
programs and data base applications as well as performing analysis, design,
and implementation of routine programs or data base applications.  The
programmer may also be involved with non-technical duties such as training
users and providing on-going support in the areas of hardware, software,
local area networks, programs, and applications.


Required skills:
The ideal candidate will possess the following qualifications:
Bachelor's degree in computer science or related field.  Additional
programming training/skill/experience may substitute for educational
requirement.  One+ year of web-based computer programming experience.

Required Skills:
Perl, SQL, Unix, JavaScript, mod_perl, HTML, CSS, project planning,
technical writing & documentation, structured application development,
product evaluation/analysis, strong English skills, ability to
work/communicate well with non-technical personnel.

Preferred Skills:
Linux, Solaris, Apache, Embperl, XML, project management, additional years
of experience programming in other languages.

Additionally, a familiarity with C/C++, PostgreSQL, Sybase, common desktop
operating systems (Windows, Mac), search engines, statistical analysis (log
files), Java, PHP and library systems (Horizon/Dynix) are beneficial.
