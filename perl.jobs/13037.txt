Description:
We are seeking a motivated and capable bioinformatician/programmer to join
the HGNC (HUGO Gene Nomenclature Committee) at the European Bioinformatics
Institute (EBI) on the prestigious Wellcome Trust Genome Campus in Hinxton,
Cambridge in the UK.
 
HGNC (www.genenames.org) is the only worldwide authority assigning
standardised human gene symbols and names,  and has three key goals;
providing unique standardised nomenclature for every human gene; ensuring
this information is freely available, widely disseminated and universally
used; and most recently, ensuring this nomenclature is expanded and
utilised across vertebrate genomes. This third aim will be the focus on
this role, creating a relational database and web portal for curating and
displaying standardised gene names across multiple vertebrate species.
 
For further information please go to www.ebi.ac.uk

Required skills:
The ideal candidate will have a proven track record in bioinformatics and
be educated to MSc or PhD level (or equivalent) in biological science or a
related subject, preferably with knowledge of phylogenetics and existing
bioinformatic tools such as BLAST. An excellent working knowledge of
relational databases and website design is required and experience of some
or all of the following would be a distinct advantage: MySQL, PostgreSQL,
Perl CGI, CMS (Drupal), jQuery.
 
The post holder will enjoy working in a team environment and ideally have
experience of collaborating with external groups, and will possess
excellent communication, organisational and interpersonal skills. This post
may involve international travel.
