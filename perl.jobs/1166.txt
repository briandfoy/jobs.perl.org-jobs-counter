Description:
About us:  

GreatSchools, Inc., and its online media property – GreatSchools.net, is
the nation's premier provider of K-12 school information. Our mission is to
help parents choose the best school for their children, support their
children’s education and improve schools. We are an Internet-based
nonprofit organization made possible by the generous support of foundations
and corporations, including the Pisces Foundation, the William and Flora
Hewlett Foundation, Washington Mutual, Inc. and the New Schools Venture
Fund.

Our organization’s dedicated and talented team is headquartered in the SOMA
area of downtown San Francisco. Our online guide to K-12 schools will serve
about 8 million users this year and is accessed at www.greatschools.net and
through co-branded partnerships with a growing list of major media
properties, including Yahoo, AOL, MSN, and the Los Angeles Times. 

Job description:  

We seek an experienced and creative leader who is excited by technical
challenges such as expanding the content of GreatSchools.net, improving Web
site usability, adding new features, developing paid subscription products
and private label Web sites, expanding the delivery of our email
newsletters, enhancing internal report capabilities, and maintaining
excellent system performance.  The Director of Technology will report to
the organization’s Chief Operating Officer and work closely with the other
members of our senior management team.	 

Primary responsibilities:

· Design, enhance and maintain a flexible, highly scalable database-driven
Web site using Open Source technologies
· Manage and mentor a team of three full-time software engineers and two
part-time systems administrators
· Develop tested, user-friendly Web application software
· Manage technical projects to tight feature release schedules
· Work closely with the GreatSchools.net team to devise and implement an
evolving vision of the site
· Oversee systems administration
· Develop and manage our internal traffic reporting systems


Required skills:
· Demonstrated leadership and project management skills
· Minimum of 5 years experience managing technical teams
· Excellent strategic planning and analytical skills 
· Knowledge of a variety of software development methodologies, with the
demonstrated ability to carry them out in practice 
· Extensive hands-on experience in Web site development
· Thorough knowledge of Internet technologies and Web site architecture
· At least 10 years programming experience, including C, Perl, HTML, and
JavaScript (expertise in Perl, especially needed)
· An understanding of Unix systems and IP network administration
· Experience with Windows NT Server, 98, 2000, and XP
· Demonstrated ability to communicate with non-technical staff regarding
technical issues
· Highly organized, responsible, and attentive to timelines and details
· Flexible and able to work in a fast-changing environment
· Commitment to the organization’s mission of helping parents make better
decisions about their children’s education


Desired skills:
· Experience with Apache, MySQL, Linux, FreeBSD, and XML is highly
desirable
· Experience with Mac OS X helpful
