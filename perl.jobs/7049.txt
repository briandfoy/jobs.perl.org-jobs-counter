Description:
The Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute uses large-scale sequencing,
informatics and analysis of genetic variation to understand gene function
in health and disease and to generate resources of lasting value.

The Sanger Institute controls one of the largest academic web presences in
the world.  Its 20+ websites take in excess of 12 million hits each week
transferring over 200GB of data to researchers worldwide.  The IT
underpinning this operation is both leading edge and world-class, utilising
very large scale high performance computing.  The websites are fed data
from large 1000+ computer farms and make use of the 700TB of SAN storage
presented over our networks.  These requirements satisfy both the 1000+
campus staff as well as the academic and industry research communities
worldwide.


Required skills:
As a web developer in the Web team you will be involved in maintaining and
further developing this world-class web service by implementation of high
quality, user-friendly web code and interfaces to support the scientific
web services. You will ensure that the Institute's data resources are made
available worldwide via the numerous Sanger-hosted websites.  Party to
this, you will be engaged in configuring and deploying new and existing web
hardware.  Good communication skills are essential as the role involves
liaising with lab scientists and their groups.
Qualifications:
Computer Science/Bioinformatics degree or extensive relevant experience.
Experience of working with the following is essential: 
PERL programming language (including object oriented software design) in a
Unix/Linux environment (although due consideration will be given to
applicants with proven skills in PHP and Java), MySQL, Apache Webserver
software, web configuration and troubleshooting, HTML/CSS/Javascript/AJAX.
Experience of working with the following is desirable:
A good knowledge of Linux systems, a good understanding of web security,
SSL, web systems administration and web architecture design.
