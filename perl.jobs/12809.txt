Description:
Northern New Mexico is a beautiful place to live in. Located in northern
New Mexico, Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL) is a multidisciplinary
research institution engaged in strategic science on behalf of national
security.  LANL enhances national security by ensuring the safety and
reliability of the U.S. nuclear stockpile, developing technologies to
reduce threats from weapons of mass destruction, and solving problems
related to energy, environment, infrastructure, health, and global security
concerns.

Theoretical Division’s T-6 Theoretical Biology and Biophysics group is
seeking a graduate research assistant (GRA) in bioinformatics and
web/database developing fields. The candidate will become part of a team of
scientists and scientific programmers who maintain, expand and do research
on HIV sequence and immunology databases and the associated websites. 

The HIV database team at Los Alamos National Laboratory has been stably
funded for more than 20 years. The team maintains a website that gives
access to a number of databases containing sequence and immunological
information on the HIV virus and several other viruses. In addition, the
team published numerous research papers and has created or provided a large
number of tools for analysis of HIV and other viral sequence and
immunological data. 
 
The team consists of around 15 people, students, postdocs, researchers and
developers. We currently have an opening for a GRA, who will be responsible
for the maintenance of a number of tools, developing new tools that operate
in the current environment, interacting with the databases, and
participating in the research projects by creating data analysis scripts in
close collaboration with the team scientists.

Required skills:
The job requires ability to work independently, excellent coding skills, an
understanding of bioinformatics issues, and frequent communication with all
other team members. 
 
Required:
-	Experience designing and creating databases and web interfaces
-	Extensive programming experience in Perl or a similar language
-	Interest in new database and development technology
-	Experience with Unix/Linux
-	Demonstrated ability to write clean, accessible, annotated code and
documentation.

Education: Bachelor’s or M.Sc degree in computer science or bioinformatics
or equivalent combination of education and experience. A cumulative GPA of
at least 3.2 the semester before hire is required. 
 
This position is a good way to take a year to work and learn while
considering enrolling in a graduate program towards M.Sc or Ph.D degrees;
applicants interested in co-enrollment in a graduate program they have
already begun are welcome.

Desired skills:
-	Background in bioinformatics
-	Background in biology, especially virology, genetics and immunology

-	Background is statistics 
-	Knowledge of R
-	Knowledge of biological databases, especially Genbank
-	Knowledge of object oriented programming and object databases
