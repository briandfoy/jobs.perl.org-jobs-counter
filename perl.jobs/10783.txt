Description:
The DOE Joint Genome Institute (JGI) in Walnut Creek, CA has an exciting
opportunity available for an experienced Software Developer. Will serve as
a team lead to allocate team resources, assign work to team members and
manage the provision of support for existing production software systems
for the management of sequence data. Will be responsible for planning and
managing the design, implementation, testing, and deployment of the
overhaul of the existing systems. Will also provide hands-on software
development support to the Production Informatics Group. Position reports
to the Production Informatics group lead and will interface with other team
leads.	http://www.jgi.doe.gov/

Initially, the majority of duties will focus on planning, coordinating, and
managing the activities of the team, with direct software development
constituting 10-20% of the role.

Specific Job Duties

Team Leadership-
- Manage the support of the existing production Sequence Data Management
systems, including the resolution of custom user and data retrieval
requests.
- Allocate team resources and assign work to team members to support
customer requests and provide prompt resolution of data entry errors,
software malfunctions, and other system needs.
- Plan and manage the design, implementation, testing, and deployment of
the overhauled versions of the Sequence Data Management systems.
- Coordinate team activities with the other Production Informatics team
leads, the Production Informatics group lead, and other JGI groups.
- Coordinate the JGI's sequence data generation needs with external
institutions (e.g. NERSC) and vendors (e.g. Illumina).
- Plan and manage the sequence data computing resource needs.

Software Development-
- Gather feedback and requirements to develop software for the Production
Informatics Group.
- Develop software using a mix of the following: Perl, Perl/CGI,
Javascript, CVS, Ruby, Ruby (with Rails), Java, PL/SQL.
- Follow prescribed practices for software version control, testing, and
release engineering.

Required skills:
- B.S. in Computer Science or a related scientific field (or equivalent
experience) and a minimum of five years related experience.
- Demonstrated experience leading a team of software developers in a
customer service oriented environment.
- Demonstrated success designing and implementing systems and software in a
scientific or technical environment, e.g., University, National Laboratory,
corporate R&D center or automated manufacturing facility.
- Full life-cycle software development experience with the ability to work
with scientific and administrative customers to define needs and
priorities.
- Demonstrated experience with Perl and experience with database design and
performance tuning in MySQL.
- Ability to apply analytical skills and creativity to solve diverse and
challenging problems.
- Effective time management, organization, and planning skills with the
ability to allocate resources and assign work to meet goals and deadlines.
- Talent for working well under short deadlines and handling multiple tasks
simultaneously.
- Ability to work effectively in a diverse team environment.


Desired skills:
- Demonstrated experience with at least some of the following: MySQL,
Perl/CGI, Javascript, Ruby (with Rails), Java, PL/SQL, CVS, Subversion.
- Experience developing web-based GUIs.
- Experience working with lab information management systems (LIMS).
- Experience working with bioinformatics tools and/or analysis techniques.
