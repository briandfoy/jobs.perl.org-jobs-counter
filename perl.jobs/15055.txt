Estée Lauder Companies Online (ELC Online) is the online division of the
Estée Lauder Companies Inc. family of brands.  Composed of passionate

e-commerce ELC Online supports Estée Lauder, MAC, Clinique, Origins, Crème

Summary

The Online
Engineer will report to the Lead Engineer, and by working with engineers
locally and globally will contribute to the development of a growing
portfolio of more than 100 commerce sites. This person is responsible for
the implementation of site redesigns, as well as developing new features
and enhancements to existing web sites and new business launches. We are
looking for a driven and successful programmer with a proven history of
working in the digital industry.

In ELC EMEA Online Engineering, you will
work alongside highly experienced and talented Designers and Producers. The
role will involve pre-build analysis, guiding the business on how to get
the most out of their brief, development and documentation through to
post-launch support and updates. You will support development fixes as part
of the QA cycle, as advised by the QA team.
The successful candidate will
be responsible for looking beyond the project in hand towards creating an
efficient and sustainable build approach, reusing code to quickly turn
around projects across multiple high profile brands in key markets across
Europe, the Middle East and Africa.
To succeed, candidates must have the
ability to prioritise their time effectively, to deliver projects on time
and to specification.  You will have good written, verbal and interpersonal
communication skills, and a sharp attention to detail.

The applicant will
be fully up to date with the latest in digital innovation; the kind of
person for whom tinkering with technical ideas is a common out of hours
practise. They will make use of allocated personal innovation time to
experiment with new technologies, approaches and concepts to grow the
business. They will also be expected to form strong relationships with
peers in our Global team, and be comfortable working in a collaborative
fashion to achieve common goals.

Candidates will be expected to provide
out of hours support on a rota basis, approximately once every 8 weeks,
will be willing to occasionally work extended hours when required, and be
available to support deployments outside of business hours when needed.
