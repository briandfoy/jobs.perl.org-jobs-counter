Description:
The Tufts University Sciences Knowledgebase, TUSK, is a dynamic multimedia
knowledge management system, which supports faculty and students in
teaching and learning.	TUSK provides a portal to an integrated body of
knowledge and means to personally organize the vast array of health
information through its related applications such as virtual patient
simulations, quizzes, on-line course evaluations and patient encounter logs
to name a few.	Begun in 1995, it serves all Tufts health sciences schools
and programs including the Medical, Dental and Veterinary Schools.  Our
department develops and supports the TUSK software.  Also, we have shared
our code base to other medical schools around the globe.
 

The Senior Software Engineer is responsible for:

    -Supervising a team of programmers responsible for TUSK's application
development;
    -Provides technical expertise, support, and direction to current and
future TUSK development;
    -Technical support/training of team members;
    -Meeting with users to ascertain their needs and desires;
    -Meeting with outside vendors and contractors to ensure the success of
deliverables;
    -Works with the director, system administrator, and system architect to
evaluate projects, develop strategies for future directions, schedule
resources, and monitor project timelines;
    -Works with other university departments or delegate’s liaison
functions to other technical staff;
    -Works on simultaneous projects;
    -Assists in defining applications and best practices as they relate to
TUSK application development;
    -Support TUSK external partners as needed.
