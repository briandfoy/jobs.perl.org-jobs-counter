Description:
Tufts University Science Knowledgebase, TUSK, is an innovative curriculum
repository/knowledge management system that serves the health science
schools at Tufts University. In additions to its knowledge management
functions it interfaces with administrative systems to provide students
with course schedules, personal folders and on-line course evaluation. New
initiatives will grow the TUSK infrastructure in several directions; to
better serve the needs of Tufts users, to interface with external data
repositories and digital libraries, and to develop and adapt TUSK
technology in accordance with developing open source standards. The
Programmer Analyst/Systems Administrator manages the TUSK and Tufts-OCW
servers, which are built by the University Systems Group, USG, and
jumpstart service. Builds and updates application and library packages
needed to run TUSK, proactively resolves issues with servers, creates
scripts to automate maintenance processes, configures new or rebuilt TUSK
servers, adds hardware and works with USG to have machines built and
patched. Schedules downtime for server updates and performs updates during
the service window. Serves as an integral part of installing TUSK in remote
locations, and works on preparing TUSK code to be more easily configurable
for remotes installations. Interacts with TUSK staff and USG to maintain
and update servers. Develops user-friendly web-based applications within
the Tufts University Science Knowledgebase – a web application backed by
SQL databases, Apache web server and Perl programming. Creates enhanced
functionality and easier user interaction for TUSK users. Provides Unix and
database support for TUSK and assists TUSK staff in developing support and
training materials for faculty.

Required skills:
Requires a Bachelor’s Degree in computer science or have relevant computer
programming experience. Must have expertise in Apache, MySQL, Perl
applications, and Solaris operating systems. Must be knowledgeable of
Mason, HTML, CSS, Javascript, XSLT, and embperl. Should be enthusiastic
about learning new technologies. Must be able to work independently and on
a team, have friendly interaction with users, and meet deadlines. Must have
superior technical, organizational, and interpersonal skills.
