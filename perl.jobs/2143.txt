Description:
The SAP IV/Technical Lead reports to the Director of the Arnold Digital
Library and Internet Services, and serves as the department’s technical
lead. Supervises the department’s technical team staff and all departmental
technology. Provides project management, routine to complex systems
analysis and design, database design, and Web programming support to the
Arnold Digital Library and Internet Services department. The incumbent
determines the technical needs and requirements for the department,
including the Center's Web presence. Carries out, supervises, or
collaborates on the technical aspects of developing and operating the
Center's Web site, the department’s servers, personal computers and
networked peripherals. 
Job duties will include: 


Advise department on technical direction and strategic planning. Monitor
technology opportunities, changes, problems and developments. Generate
project ideas and proposals. 

Direct the work of the technical team. Coordinate or oversee technical
projects, directing system administrators, programmer/developers and
desktop support personnel as necessary, monitoring progress, and work with
the Director to manage priorities and timelines. 

Supervise technical team personnel, including performance, training,
disciplinary issues, mentoring and resource allocation. 

Maintain a thorough knowledge of department in order to anticipate and
accommodate all technical needs. Assist Director in creating technical
budget requests. 

Develop and maintain technical standards, policies and procedures. Oversee
the creation and maintenance of departmental technology-related
documentation. 

Maintain current knowledge of and comply with Center-wide standards and
policies regarding the use of hardware, software and network resources. 

Represent department and/or team at various meetings, including
participation in the department’s lead team. 

Acquire, maintain, analyze and report statistics relevant to department. 

Lead, or participate in, departmental and Center-wide computer security and
access control solutions. 

Run meetings and give written or oral presentations as necessary. 

Train staff, develop training materials or assist with training. 

Confer with users and technical staff to determine programming and database
requirements, maintaining on-going communication through programming
completion. 

Analyze user requirements, providing advice to users on the implications of
analysis and programming applications. 

Estimate and prioritize programming requests, creating plans to meet
timelines, and soliciting input from Director and other staff as necessary.


Design and implement routine to complex programs and database applications
to meet user specifications. 

Create logic descriptions using accepted documentation tools and methods
and translate logic specifications into coded computer instructions. 

Modify existing programs and database applications. 

Acquire, maintain and customize Web-based tools. 

Test and debug new and modified programs and database applications. 

Maintain documentation of all programs and applications. 

Perform quality control procedures on programs and applications. 
General Technical Duties 

Generate a variety of reports as required. 

Perform hardware/software support responsibilities such as training users,
installing and configuring hardware/software and associated equipment,
keeping records of hardware/software inventory, coordinating requests for
repairs and maintenance, and recommending equipment modifications or
additions as necessary. 

Troubleshoot user problems. 

Other duties or projects as assigned or as necessary to support the
operations of the department.
