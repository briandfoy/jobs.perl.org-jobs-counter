Description:
The Influenza Sequence Database at Los Alamos National Laboratory is a
collection of genetic sequence data for the flu virus, from around the
world.	We use Perl extensively in gathering and curating our data, and
Perl is the server-side coding language used in our website, via mod_perl. 
We're looking for someone to assist in maintaining and extending the site,
and to carry on with writing and running the code to update and clean the
database.  There's a lot going on in our project, so there will be
opportunities to make original contributions, through developing new tools
& new interfaces.

Required skills:
Solid knowledge of Perl, preferably in a Web context.  Unix know-how. 
Grasp of relational DB concepts.  Some knowledge of what makes a good web
interface, and how to build one.  Ability to solve problems, well.  Ability
to communicate, well, and to work both with others & alone.

Desired skills:
Familiarity with molecular biology.  Sysadmin experience.  Experience
designing & implementing databases.  Knowledge of Flash, especially
Actionscript.  Experience coding in a mod_perl environment.
