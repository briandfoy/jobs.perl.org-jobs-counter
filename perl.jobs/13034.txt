Description:
TUSK, the Tufts University Sciences Knowledgebase, is a comprehensive open
source educational system serving health sciences graduate education.
Started in 1995, TUSK is now used in 6 US medical schools and 11
universities in the developing world. As part of the USAID funded REPOND
project in conjunction with the Cummings School of Veterinary Medicine, the
Office of Technology for Learning in the Health Sciences, TLHS, which
produces TUSK is helping universities to respond to emerging pandemic
threat. TLHS has many service agreements to assist in the installation and
training of TUSK which brings occasional foreign travel.
The Senior System Administrator is responsible for building and maintaining
the TUSK servers in a manner consistent with the application. The key
responsibilities include:
•	Installing/upgrading the hardware and server software, 
•	Occasional travel to international sites to install and train staff

•	Planning and making recommendations for future hardware and space
needs, 
•	Designing and implementing a disaster recovery plan, 
•	Troubleshooting system problems, 
•	Working with the development staff to keep software versions
current, 
•	Installing and maintaining TUSK systems at our partner schools
domestically and internationally, 
The Sr. Systems Administrator must understand the TUSK application and
consider the developers and other TUSK staff as his/her clients by
consulting and providing documentation on all systems. The administrator
must be able to work with other TUSK administrators in a coordinated manner
to serve the needs of the TUSK application, and work with the Tufts
departments including UIT and its divisions, as appropriate

Required skills:
Bachelor's Degree with a minimum of 5 years of experience or High School
Diploma and equivalent with 10+years of experience. Must have Linux Systems
administration experience and experience with open source tools, including
virtualization with KVM and libvert. Have expertise in MySQL; Apache; and
Perl. Technical background in a multi-platform, multi-protocol production
environment. Demonstrated experience in the following areas installation,
configuration, tuning, security, backup and recovery. Demonstrated
experience with programming and scripting languages, especially perl.
Excellent problem resolution, analytical and, verbal and written
communication skills particularly with respect to creating and maintaining
documentation. Ability to work with people at all levels within the
university; ability to work with vendors; and ability to work independently
and in groups. 
Preferred Qualifications: 
MS in a related field. Experience with database administration. Experience
with Eucalyptus, OpenStack or Cloudstack. In-depth expertise with puppet or
chef.
Special Work Schedule Requirements: 
May be asked to perform occasional server maintenance, upgrades, and
problems during non-business hours.
